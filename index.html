Executive Summary
Part A — Data Protection: Japan's privacy framework comprises 4 primary statutes, 6 core PPC guidelines, 6 Q&A documents, 13 sector-specific guidelines (finance, medical, telecom) with 4 telecom commentaries, 4 My Number guidelines, and 8 supplementary documents — 41 entries issued by PPC — many jointly with sector ministries (FSA, METI, MHLW, MIC, MOJ).
Part B — AI Governance: Japan enacted its first AI law (AI推進法, Act No. 53/2025) effective September 2025, establishing an AI Strategy Headquarters under the Prime Minister. The cross-ministerial AI Business Operator Guidelines v1.1 (METI+MIC, March 2025) and the Digital Agency's generative AI procurement guideline (DS-920) form the operational backbone, complemented by sector-specific AI guidelines and 4 AISI (AI Safety Institute) technical framework documents covering safety evaluation, red teaming, data quality, and CAIO governance.
The APPI was comprehensively amended in 2021 (phased implementation: national agencies April 2022, local governments April 2023) to unify private and public sector rules. Japan holds EU/UK adequacy decisions. The AI framework follows an innovation-first, soft-law approach — no penalties in the AI Promotion Act itself — with sector regulators issuing binding guidance where needed.
The Hiroshima AI Process (G7, 2023) represents Japan's leadership in international AI governance, with 60 participating countries/regions and a reporting framework operational since February 2025.
This catalog covers 73 entries across both domains (41 data protection, 32 AI governance), with select entries bundling closely related companion documents, enabling international organizations to navigate Japan's complete data protection and AI governance landscape in a single reference.
Chapter 01
Laws (法令)
01
Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI)
個人情報の保護に関する法律 · 개인정보보호법
平成15年法律第57号250 articles259KB markdown
elaws.e-gov.go.jp — lawid=415AC0000000057
Japan's comprehensive data protection law governing the handling of personal information by both private business operators and administrative organs. Enacted in 2003 and substantially amended in 2015, 2020, and 2022 to address evolving data processing needs, cross-border transfers, and public sector integration.
Practical Significance
The foundational statute for all privacy compliance in Japan. The 2021 amendment (enacted 2021, phased implementation) unified private and public sector rules under one act — national agencies/independent administrative agencies from April 2022, local governments from April 2023 — eliminating the need to consult separate public sector laws. Defines core concepts: personal information, personal-related information (個人関連情報), individual identification codes, and special care-required personal information.
02
APPI Enforcement Order
個人情報の保護に関する法律施行令 · 개인정보보호법 시행령
平成15年政令第507号40 articles64KB markdown
elaws.e-gov.go.jp — lawid=415CO0000000507
Cabinet order providing detailed specifications for APPI provisions. Specifies individual identification codes (biometrics, passport numbers, driver's license numbers), categories of retained personal data exemptions, and details cross-border transfer requirements.
Practical Significance
Provides the operational detail absent from the statute itself. Critical for determining whether specific data qualifies as individual identification codes, which countries are recognized as having equivalent protection, and numeric thresholds triggering enhanced obligations.
03
APPI Enforcement Rules
個人情報の保護に関する法律施行規則 · 개인정보보호법 시행규칙
平成28年個人情報保護委員会規則第3号70 articles91KB markdown
elaws.e-gov.go.jp — lawid=428M60020000003
PPC rules specifying procedural requirements for consent, notification, record-keeping, breach reporting, and cross-border transfer documentation. Provides templates and formats for mandatory notifications and reports.
Practical Significance
Contains the most granular compliance requirements. Essential for implementing breach notification procedures (including the prompt preliminary report (approximately 3–5 business days) and 30-day full report deadlines (60 days for certain third-party provision breaches)), consent records for cross-border transfers, and opt-out notification procedures.
04
Act on the Use of Numbers to Identify a Specific Individual (My Number Act)
行政手続における特定の個人を識別するための番号の利用等に関する法律 · 마이넘버법
平成25年法律第27号331KB markdown
elaws.e-gov.go.jp — lawid=425AC0000000027
Governs the use of individual numbers (My Number / マイナンバー) for administrative procedures including tax, social security, and disaster response. Imposes stricter handling requirements than the APPI for specific personal information containing My Numbers.
Practical Significance
Any employer, financial institution, or service provider handling My Numbers must comply with heightened security and use-limitation requirements. Cross-border transfer of My Number data is essentially prohibited. Penalties for misuse are significantly more severe than APPI violations.
Chapter 02
Core Guidelines (ガイドライン)
05
Guidelines on the APPI — General Rules
個人情報の保護に関する法律についてのガイドライン（通則編） · 통칙편
最終改正: 令和7年6月489KB markdown · 189 pages
ppc.go.jp — guidelines01.pdf
The most comprehensive and important guideline. Provides clause-by-clause interpretation of the APPI for private business operators, covering definitions (personal information, personal data, retained personal data), utilization purpose, acquisition, maintenance/management, third-party provision, disclosure/correction/suspension, and breach notification procedures.
Practical Significance
The single most important compliance reference. At 189 pages, it provides authoritative interpretive guidance with practical examples for every major APPI obligation. Must be the first document consulted for any data processing question. Includes detailed guidance on the 2022 amendments including personal-related information (個人関連情報) and enhanced breach reporting to PPC with data subject notification.
06
Guidelines on the APPI — Cross-Border Third-Party Transfers
同（外国にある第三者への提供編） · 외국 제3자 제공편
最終改正: 令和7年12月76KB markdown
ppc.go.jp — guidelines02.pdf
Detailed guidance on the requirements for transferring personal data to third parties located in foreign countries. Covers the three lawful bases: consent with information provision, transfers to countries with equivalent systems, and transfers to parties with equivalent measures via contract or group rules (APEC CBPR, etc.).
Practical Significance
Essential for any multinational operation. The 2022 amendment significantly enhanced information disclosure requirements for cross-border transfers, requiring business operators (個人情報取扱事業者) to provide the destination country's privacy regime details, the recipient's safeguards, and regular updates. Non-compliance exposes organizations to PPC enforcement action and reputational damage.
07
Guidelines on the APPI — Third-Party Provision Confirmation and Record-Keeping
同（第三者提供時の確認・記録義務編） · 제3자 제공시 확인·기록의무편
最終改正: 令和5年12月70KB markdown
ppc.go.jp — guidelines03.pdf
Specifies the record-keeping obligations for both providers and recipients of personal data in third-party transfers. Details what records must be created, their content requirements, retention periods, and the methods for confirming the lawful acquisition of received data.
Practical Significance
Creates an audit trail for personal data flows. Organizations receiving personal data from third parties must verify the data's lawful origin and maintain records — failure to do so creates compliance exposure even if the data itself was lawfully collected by the provider.
08
Guidelines on the APPI — Pseudonymized and Anonymized Information
同（仮名加工情報・匿名加工情報編） · 가명·익명가공정보편
最終改正: 令和6年12月26KB markdown
ppc.go.jp — guidelines04.pdf
Guidance on the creation, handling, and provision of pseudonymized information (仮名加工情報) and anonymized information (匿名加工情報). Defines processing standards, permissible uses, safety measures, and restrictions on re-identification. The 2020 amendment (effective April 2022) introduced pseudonymized information as a new data category enabling internal analysis without some APPI restrictions.
Practical Significance
Pseudonymized information enables internal data analytics and AI training without full APPI consent requirements — but cannot be provided to third parties. Anonymized information can be shared externally but must meet rigorous de-identification standards. Understanding the distinction is critical for data utilization strategies.
09
Guidelines on the APPI — Accredited Personal Information Protection Organizations
同（認定個人情報保護団体編） · 인정개인정보보호단체편
最終改正: 令和7年12月60KB markdown
ppc.go.jp — nintei_dantai_guidelines.pdf
Outlines the accreditation standards, operational obligations, and complaint handling procedures for organizations accredited by PPC to promote personal information protection within their industry. Covers the establishment of personal information protection guidelines (自主ルール) and complaint mediation systems.
Practical Significance
Industry associations seeking accreditation — or member companies of accredited organizations — must understand these requirements. Accredited organizations can establish sector-specific rules that are more stringent than the APPI, creating additional compliance obligations for their members.
10
Guidelines on the APPI — Administrative Organs
同（行政機関等編） · 행정기관등편
最終改正: 令和7年12月187KB markdown
ppc.go.jp — koutekibumon_guidelines.pdf
Comprehensive guidance for administrative organs, independent administrative agencies, and local governments on personal information handling under APPI Chapter 5. Covers unique public sector obligations including individual information files, information disclosure, restrictions on purpose-external use, and online linking with other administrative organs.
Practical Significance
Mandatory reference for government agencies and their contractors handling personal data. Post-2022 unification, local governments that previously operated under ordinances must now comply with the unified APPI framework, making this guideline essential for transition compliance.
